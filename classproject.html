Chunking Science

MMC 5015 Your Digital Life Project

As a graduate student in the science and health communication track and as a writer in the University of Florida Office of Research, I have a strong interest in science news.

Writing about science is a lot like newspaper reporting, it takes you places you wouldn’t have gone otherwise. I’ve flown with a weed scientist over a nature preserve being swallowed up by invasive vines, gone along with a wildlife researcher tagging barn owls and hiked through saw palmettos with a scientist studying their value as a pharmaceutical crop. I’ve held a bat and observed an alligator necropsy. Science is often fun, almost always interesting, and I almost always come away wishing I had paid more attention in long-ago chemistry and physics classes (the researchers likely wish this, too).

From climate change to the ways we mine big data to the anti-science that rears its head in policy circles, science is a hot topic, so that is my class project for MMC 5015 Your Digital Life, hopefully “chunked” appropriately per the professor’s instructions.
